Helping women in rural Guizhou learn, grow, and lead.

Who we are

A social club rooted in learning and community.

She Learns Program brings together students, volunteers, local mentors, donors, and collaborators to support the education of women in rural Guizhou. We aim to make learning welcoming, accessible, and empowering through safe spaces, books, workshops, and practical resources.

Literacy Support Digital Skills Community Mentorship Women-Led Learning
Our vision

Education that opens futures.

We believe that when women gain access to education, entire communities benefit. Our vision is to strengthen confidence, opportunity, and independence by building meaningful educational pathways that are locally supported and culturally respectful.

What we do

Programs designed for access, confidence, and growth.

Our initiatives blend formal and informal learning so women can engage in ways that suit their lives, responsibilities, and ambitions.

📚

Learning Circles

Small community-based classes focused on reading, writing, language, and peer support in welcoming local spaces.

💻

Digital Skills

Practical lessons in computer basics, online safety, communication tools, and digital confidence for modern opportunities.

🌱

Mentorship & Wellbeing

Guidance from volunteers and mentors that supports confidence-building, long-term aspirations, and personal growth.

🧵

Creative Workshops

Hands-on sessions that combine storytelling, art, cultural knowledge, and practical making as part of learning.

🏫

Resource Hubs

Book corners, shared materials, and flexible spaces where women can study, gather, and continue learning together.

🤝

Volunteer Exchange

Meaningful volunteer opportunities that connect skilled supporters with rural learning communities and local leaders.

Our impact

Support that reaches beyond the classroom.

We focus on education as a pathway to confidence, participation, and greater choice. By supporting women with practical tools and community connection, the program creates benefits that extend to families and villages.

01

Improved access to learning

Flexible local programs reduce barriers and bring educational support closer to where women live.

02

Confidence and capability

Participants build practical literacy and digital skills that support study, work, and everyday independence.

03

Stronger community networks

Learning groups become spaces for solidarity, mentorship, and sustained local leadership among women.
